Reading the wheels

The major scale steps go 2·2·1·2·2·2·1 semitones — seven notes with two small (1-semitone) gaps, filling the octave fairly evenly (a seven-sided shape). The 宫-mode (gōng) pentatonic steps go 2·2·3·2·3 — five notes with no semitones at all; the two 3-semitone gaps (where fa and ti would be) give it its open, gapped character (a five-sided shape). Removing those two leading-tone tendencies is a big part of why the pentatonic sounds distinctly Chinese rather than Western. Tonic = C (1=宫); audio is a simple sine synth.
